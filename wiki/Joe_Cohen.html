He's given consultations to over 1000 people who have sought his health advice. He's written hundreds of science posts, multiple books on improving health, and speaks at various health conferences.

He's building a team of top scientists who will improve the level of accuracy of health content on the web. He's also founded SelfDecode and LabTestAnalyzer, popular genetic and lab software tools to improve health.

Proprietary Model vs. Open Source Model

In your campaign investor panel video, you focus and highlight the intellectual property that Genius Labs owns and is continuing to develop. This relatively closed and proprietary model for innovation is currently nearly universal within the digital health industry. My question is, is the model optimal or required for innovation, or is it possible that a more open source and collaborative model such as the of WordPress could produce more value faster for everyone by eliminating the duplication of programmer effort that occurs when multiple companies are building the exact same features? Despite being open source, WordPress has a $3 billion valuation. This value is derived from the services it provides which themselves are made more valuable by the free and open source contributions by the developer ecosystem. So it's able to transcend the zero-sum game of winners and losers allowing everyone to be a winner by participating. If you don't think an open source is optimal or desirable path in the private digital health sector, do you believe that publicly-funded projects like All of Us at the NIH should be required to open source their code?
