Mike P. Sinn

Mike P. Sinn is a software engineer who created Crowdsourcing Cures to reduce suffering by helping individuals, nonprofits, businesses, and government work together to:

1. Create a global anonymous database of longitudinal data on symptom severity and factors that could influence them
2. Analyze this data to reveal the positive and negative effects of different foods, drugs, and supplements on the severity and development of disease
3. Make this data available to individuals in the form of Outcome Labels that illustrate these effects.

He also built QuantiModo a platform to aggregate 14 million data points from thousands of people and analyze them. He used that data to generate 90,000 studies on the relationships between various factors and the severity of chronic illnesses such as depression. So that was pretty cool.

He possesses a decade of engineering management experience at St. Louis Testing Laboratories using data gathering and analysis to solve problems in a number of industries.

He created ThinkByNumbers.org in an effort to realize a world that uses data to drive public policy which maximizes gross national health and happiness.
